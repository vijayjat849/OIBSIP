Christopher Nolan

A leading filmmaker of the 21st century

Here's a time line of Christopher Nolan

  • 1970 - Christopher Nolan is born in London, England.
  • 1989 - Nolan directs his first film, a short titled "Tarantella," while studying English Literature at University College London.
  • 1998 - Nolan writes and directs his feature film debut, "Following," a low-budget neo-noir thriller.
  • 2000 - Nolan gains critical acclaim with "Memento," a psychological thriller that establishes his signature nonlinear storytelling style.
  • 2002 - He directs "Insomnia," a remake of the Norwegian film, marking his first major studio production.
  • 2005 - Nolan helms "Batman Begins," rebooting the Batman film franchise and receiving praise for his fresh take on the superhero genre.
  • 2008 - Nolan's "The Dark Knight" becomes a blockbuster hit, earning critical acclaim and setting numerous box office records.
  • 2010 - He directs and writes the highly acclaimed sci-fi action film "Inception," exploring dreams and reality.
  • 2012 - Nolan concludes his Batman trilogy with "The Dark Knight Rises," receiving positive reviews and concluding his impactful take on the iconic character.
  • 2014 - He directs and co-writes "Interstellar," a visually stunning and thought-provoking space exploration film.
  • 2017 - Nolan directs "Dunkirk," a war film based on the evacuation of Allied soldiers during World War II, earning critical acclaim and multiple awards
  • 2020 - Nolan releases "Tenet," an intricate time-bending spy thriller that showcases his signature complex storytelling style.
  • 2023 - Oppenheimer is an upcoming biographical thriller film written and directed by Christopher Nolan, based on the 2005 book American Prometheus by Kai Bird and Martin J. Sherwin.
